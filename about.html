Each Night half a million people in United States are home less.

  • Some sleep in shelters, others on streets, and one quarter of them are children
  • About 15 percent are chronically homeless, means they didn't have permanent home in years. They switch between jail, hospitals, and shelters to find a place to sleep
  • the averagemonthly cost of serving a family in an emergency shelter is $4,819
  • voucher for housing, on the other hand, is just $1,162.

Why homeless people are opting out of shelter and staying on streets?

They suffered through violence, theft, or other trauma in these ostensibly safer spaces.

  • 826 “violent incidents” in New York City homeless shelters
  • sexual assault and domestic violence
  • Shelters don’t allow residents to develop a sense of permanency
  • Numerious studies showed permanency helps people get a job or stay sober
  • Affordable housing is the best solution to end this problem
  • Once they have safe home, they can look for other services
  • Utah reduced its chronically-homeless population 72 percent between 2005 and 2014 through housing-first
