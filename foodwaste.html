How Much Food is Getting Wasted in America?

Studies have found that 30%-40% of food produce in United States is thrown away.

  • According to EPA study, 38 million tons of food gets thrown away each year in America.
  • 60 million metric tons of food is wasted a year in the United States, with a value of $162 billion.
  • The amount of food thrown away in 2012, could have fed 190 million adults each day that year
  • On the other hand, millions of families struggling to have one decent meal.
  • Food discarded by retailers and consumers in developed countries would be enough to feed Worlds 870 million hungry people.

How can we connect these two issues?

Food getting wasted need to circulated among the needing and deserving people, instead of ending in trash.

  • Food waste is an environmental problem too.
  • Food wastes ends up in landfills, where it decomposes and releases methane gas.
  • Retailers and restaurants need to donate food to shelter homes or food pantries.
  • Build a system, so food can get distributed to the needy individuals.
  • Bring awareness among people and businesses.
  • Build a strong network of picking food from the businesses, and redistribute it to the hungry and poor.
